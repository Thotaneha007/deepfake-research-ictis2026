# Deepfakes: A Review of Creation and Research Trends

## Overview
This work presents a comprehensive survey of deepfake technologies, focusing on the evolution of generation methods, detection mechanisms, and emerging research trends. The study examines how advances in deep learning have enabled highly realistic synthetic media, while also introducing significant challenges in security, trust, and digital forensics.

## Abstract
Deepfake technology has experienced rapid growth due to advancements in deep learning architectures capable of generating highly realistic synthetic media. While such developments have beneficial applications in entertainment and education, they also raise serious concerns related to misinformation, identity fraud, and cybersecurity threats.

This paper provides a structured review of deepfake generation techniques, including Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), autoencoder-based models, and diffusion models. It further explores detection approaches ranging from traditional forensic analysis to deep learning-based and multimodal techniques. Additionally, the study analyzes benchmark datasets, tools, and bibliometric trends to highlight the rapid expansion of research in this domain.

## Key Contributions
- Systematic review of deepfake generation methodologies  
- Comparative analysis of detection techniques (forensic, deep learning, multimodal)  
- Discussion of societal, ethical, and cybersecurity implications  
- Overview of benchmark datasets and tools used in research  
- Bibliometric analysis of research trends (2018–2026)
